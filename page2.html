Avogadros Law

Avogadros law is defined as an experimental gas law relating volume of a gas to the amount of substance of gas present. The law explicitly states that "equal volumes of all gases, at the same temperature and pressure, have the same number of molecules.

P Is the pressure of the gas. V is the volume of the gas. N is the amount of substance (in moles). R is the ideal gas constant. Finally, T is the temperature of the gas

Ideal gas law

The ideal gas law is the equation of state of hypothetical ideal gas. This allows for approximation of gases given many conditions, although it has several limitations.
