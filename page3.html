Charles Law Example

As seen in the following image, the question gives the student the temperature and volume. You can easily deteremine that there is a before and after change. You are given volume and temperature therefore you will use Charles Law. Although you will need to rearrange the formula in order to solve for the second temperature.

V=volume and T=temperature

Step 2

Since you need to solve for Temperature 2, you must first rearrange the formula. With simple algebra, you cancel out Volume 2 and it is moved to the opposite side.

Step 3

Folllowing this, you must then perform simple math and solve the equation created. You simply do this by multiplying 15L times 28L then dividing by 298K. After this you will then retrieve temperature 2, 1.409.

Don't forget to round according to significant figure rules!
